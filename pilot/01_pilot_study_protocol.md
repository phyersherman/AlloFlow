# AlloFlow Pilot Study Protocol

**Version:** 1.0  
**Date:** March 2026  
**Principal Investigator:** Aaron Pomeranz, PsyD  
**Platform:** AlloFlow — Open-Source UDL Instructional Design Platform

---

## 1. Study Overview

### Purpose
This pilot study evaluates the effectiveness, usability, and implementation feasibility of AlloFlow — a free, open-source Universal Design for Learning (UDL) platform — in K–12 classroom settings. The study aims to generate preliminary evidence for grant funding applications (IES SBIR Phase I, Spencer Foundation) and inform future randomized controlled trials.

### Research Questions
1. **Efficacy:** Does teacher use of AlloFlow improve the quality and frequency of differentiated instruction compared to baseline practice?
2. **Usability:** How do teachers and students rate the usability, accessibility, and overall experience of AlloFlow?
3. **Feasibility:** Can AlloFlow be implemented with fidelity in diverse school settings with minimal training and support?
4. **Impact:** Does student access to AlloFlow-generated materials improve engagement, comprehension, and learning outcomes?

### Study Design
- **Type:** Pre-post quasi-experimental pilot study with mixed methods
- **Duration:** 6 weeks (1 week training + 4 weeks implementation + 1 week data collection)
- **Participants:** 5–10 teachers across 2–3 schools, with their corresponding classrooms (estimated 100–250 students)
- **Comparison:** Within-subjects pre/post design; each teacher serves as their own control

---

## 2. Participant Recruitment

### Teacher Eligibility
- Currently teaching in a K–12 setting
- Have access to a device with Google Chrome and internet connectivity
- Willing to use AlloFlow for at least 3 lessons per week during the study period
- No prior AlloFlow experience required

### Student Eligibility
- Enrolled in a participating teacher's classroom
- Ages 5–18 (grades K–12)
- Parent/guardian consent obtained (see Consent Form)
- Student assent obtained for ages 7+

### Recruitment Strategy
- Partner with 2–3 schools (ideally diverse in demographics, grade levels, and setting)
- Present at a faculty meeting or PD session
- Provide an information sheet and collect teacher consent
- Distribute parent consent forms via school communication channels

## 5. Training Protocol

### Session Format
- **Duration:** 2 hours (synchronous, in-person or virtual)
- **Materials:** AlloFlow Quick Start Guide, sample lesson files, practice scenarios

### Training Agenda
1. **Overview** (15 min) — Platform philosophy, UDL alignment, privacy architecture
2. **Core Workflow Demo** (30 min) — Source → Adapt → Glossary → Reader → Quiz → Export
3. **Hands-On Practice** (45 min) — Teachers create a lesson from their own curriculum
4. **Advanced Features** (20 min) — Adventure Mode, STEM Lab, Word Sounds, BehaviorLens
5. **Q&A + Support Plan** (10 min) — Weekly check-ins, support contact, troubleshooting

### Ongoing Support
- Weekly 15-minute check-in call with PI or research assistant
- Shared communication channel (email/Slack/Teams) for questions
- Troubleshooting guide document

## 6. Data Analysis Plan

### Quantitative Analysis
- **Pre/post comparison:** Paired t-tests or Wilcoxon signed-rank tests for survey items
- **Descriptive statistics:** Means, standard deviations, and frequencies for all survey items
- **Effect sizes:** Cohen's d for pre/post differences
- **Platform usage:** Descriptive analysis of session data

### Qualitative Analysis
- **Interview transcription and coding:** Thematic analysis using inductive coding
- **Open-ended survey responses:** Content analysis with frequency counts
- **Cross-case analysis:** Patterns across teachers, grade levels, and school settings

### Reporting
- Summary statistics table with effect sizes
- Thematic summary of qualitative findings
- Case studies of 2–3 "exemplar" implementations
- Limitations and recommendations for future research

---

## 7. Ethical Considerations

### IRB Review
- Submit to university or independent IRB for expedited review (minimal risk)
- Provide all instruments, consent forms, and data management plan

### Data Privacy
- AlloFlow stores all student data locally on the device — no cloud transmission
- Survey data collected via de-identified Google Forms or paper instruments
- No PII collected from students beyond grade level and demographic category
- All data stored on encrypted, password-protected drives
- Data retained for 3 years per federal requirements, then destroyed

### Informed Consent
- Written parent/guardian consent required for all student participants
- Student assent obtained for ages 7+
- Teacher consent includes permission to record interviews
- Participants may withdraw at any time without consequence
